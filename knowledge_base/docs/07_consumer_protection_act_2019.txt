=== CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT, 2019 ===

DEFINITIONS (S.2):
Consumer (S.2(7)): Person who buys goods or hires services for consideration; NOT for resale or commercial purpose. Includes online purchases.
Defect (S.2(10)): Any fault in quality, quantity, potency, purity or standard of goods.
Deficiency (S.2(11)): Any fault or shortcoming in the manner of performance of a service.
Unfair trade practice (S.2(47)): False representation, misleading advertisements, deceptive packaging.

Six Consumer Rights (S.2(9)):
1. Right to safety from hazardous goods/services.
2. Right to information about quality, quantity, price.
3. Right to choice among competitive goods at competitive prices.
4. Right to be heard in consumer forums.
5. Right to seek redress against unfair practices.
6. Right to consumer education.

CENTRAL CONSUMER PROTECTION AUTHORITY (CCPA) S.10-27:
Regulates unfair trade practices and misleading ads. Powers: recall products, discontinue practices, impose penalties, file class complaints.
Penalty for misleading ads: up to ₹10 lakhs first offence, ₹50 lakhs repeat.
Celebrity endorsers also held liable (S.21).

CONSUMER DISPUTES REDRESSAL COMMISSIONS (S.28-58):
District Commission: claims up to ₹1 crore.
State Commission: claims ₹1 crore to ₹10 crore; appellate over District.
National Commission: claims above ₹10 crore; appellate over State.
File online: e-daakhil portal (edaakhil.nic.in).
Fees: ₹100-2000; nil for BPL cardholders.
Limitation: 2 years from cause of action (delay can be condoned).
Relief: replacement, repair, refund, compensation, punitive damages.

PRODUCT LIABILITY (S.82-87):
Manufacturer liable for: manufacturing defect, design defect, non-conformity with express warranty.
Service provider liable for deficient service.
Seller liable if product altered without reasonable care.

E-COMMERCE RULES 2020:
Mandatory: seller name, address, customer care contact. Grievance Officer must respond in 48 hours.
Prohibited: fake reviews, preferential listing without disclosure, discriminatory pricing based on user profiling.

PRACTICAL NOTES:
Q: Where do I file a consumer complaint? A: District Commission for claims up to ₹1 crore; online via edaakhil.nic.in.
Q: What is the time limit to file? A: 2 years from cause of action.
Q: Can I appeal a District Commission order? A: Yes, to State Commission within 45 days.
Q: Is there a fee to file? A: ₹100-2000; BPL cardholders exempt.
