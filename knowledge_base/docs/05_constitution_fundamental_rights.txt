=== Fundamental Rights — Constitution of India (Part III, Articles 12-35) ===

Art.12: Definition of State — includes Central and State Governments, Parliament, State Legislatures, local authorities, and other authorities (Ajay Hasia test for instrumentality of State).

Art.13: Any law inconsistent with Fundamental Rights is void to the extent of inconsistency. Includes ordinances, bylaws, customs.

Art.14: Right to Equality — equality before law AND equal protection of laws. Reasonable classification test: intelligible differentia + rational nexus.

Art.15: No discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth. Art.15(3): special provision for women and children. Art.15(6): 10% EWS reservation (103rd Amendment 2019).

Art.16: Equality of opportunity in public employment. Art.16(4): reservations for backward classes. Art.16(4A): reservations in promotion for SC/ST.

Art.17: Abolition of untouchability — punishable offence under Protection of Civil Rights Act 1955.

Art.18: Abolition of titles. Bharat Ratna and Padma awards upheld by Supreme Court.

Art.19: Six Freedoms:
(a) Speech and expression
(b) Peaceful assembly without arms
(c) Form associations or unions
(d) Move freely throughout India
(e) Reside and settle anywhere in India
(g) Practise any profession or carry on any trade
Subject to reasonable restrictions under Art.19(2)-(6).

Art.20: Protection in respect of conviction — no ex post facto law, no double jeopardy, no self-incrimination.

Art.21: Protection of life and personal liberty. Expanded by courts to include right to privacy (K.S. Puttaswamy 2017), health, livelihood, speedy trial, legal aid.

Art.21A: Free and compulsory education for children 6-14 years (86th Amendment 2002). Implemented through Right to Education Act 2009.

Art.22: Protection against arbitrary arrest — inform grounds immediately, right to consult advocate, produce before magistrate within 24 hours.

Art.23: Prohibition of trafficking in human beings, begar, forced labour. Bonded Labour System (Abolition) Act 1976.

Art.24: No child below 14 in factories, mines, or hazardous employment.

Art.25-28: Freedom of conscience and religion; manage religious affairs; no religious tax; no religious instruction in state educational institutions.

Art.29-30: Minorities' rights to conserve language, script, culture. Right to establish and administer educational institutions.

Art.32: Right to constitutional remedies — Dr Ambedkar called this the heart and soul of the Constitution.
Five Writs: Habeas Corpus (produce detained person), Mandamus (command public duty), Prohibition (stop inferior court exceeding jurisdiction), Certiorari (quash order), Quo Warranto (by what authority held).

PRACTICAL NOTES:
Q: What is Article 21? A: Right to life and personal liberty — the most expansive FR.
Q: Can freedom of speech be restricted? A: Yes, on grounds in Art.19(2) including public order, defamation.
Q: What writ challenges illegal detention? A: Habeas Corpus.
Q: At what age does right to free education apply? A: 6 to 14 years under Art.21A.
