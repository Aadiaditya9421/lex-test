=== PROTECTION OF WOMEN FROM DOMESTIC VIOLENCE ACT, 2005 (PWDVA) ===

Aggrieved person (S.2(a)): Any woman in or having been in a domestic relationship, alleging domestic violence.
Domestic relationship (S.2(f)): Persons living/having lived together — related by consanguinity, marriage, adoption, or joint family. INCLUDES live-in relationships (D. Velusamy v. D. Patchaiammal SC 2010).
Respondent: Can be adult male member OR female relative of husband (SC ruling — mother-in-law, sister-in-law).

DOMESTIC VIOLENCE (S.3):
Physical abuse: hitting, slapping, kicking, burning.
Sexual abuse: forced intercourse, unwanted sexual acts.
Verbal and emotional abuse: insults, threats, humiliation about dowry.
Economic abuse: depriving financial resources, preventing employment, disposing assets.

ORDERS AVAILABLE:
S.18 Protection Order: prohibit respondent from committing DV; entering workplace or school.
S.19 Residence Order: respondent not to dispossess aggrieved from shared household.
S.20 Monetary Relief: maintenance, medical expenses, loss of earnings. In addition to CrPC S.125.
S.21 Custody Order: temporary custody of children.
S.22 Compensation Order: for mental torture, emotional distress, injuries.
S.23 Interim Orders: ex parte orders on affidavit for urgent relief.

PROCEDURE:
Application: by aggrieved person, Protection Officer, or any person on her behalf. NO FIR needed.
First hearing: within 3 days (S.12(4)). Disposal: within 60 days.
Breach of protection order (S.31): cognizable, non-bailable offence — up to 1 year + ₹20,000 fine.

COMPARISON WITH IPC S.498A:
IPC S.498A: criminal conviction possible; 3 years; cognizable, non-bailable.
PWDVA: civil remedy; results in protection/residence/maintenance orders; no criminal conviction.
Both can be used simultaneously.

=== PROTECTION OF CHILDREN FROM SEXUAL OFFENCES ACT, 2012 (POCSO) ===

Scope: Protects all children below 18 years (gender neutral for victims).

OFFENCES AND PUNISHMENTS:
S.3-4: Penetrative sexual assault — minimum 10 years RI, maximum life + fine.
S.5-6: Aggravated penetrative sexual assault (by police officer, teacher, relative, on child below 12) — minimum 20 years to life or death (child below 12 — 2019 amendment).
S.7-8: Sexual assault (non-penetrative) — minimum 3 years, maximum 5 years.
S.11-12: Sexual harassment (gestures, words, showing pornography) — up to 3 years.
S.13-15: Child pornography — up to 5 years; storage for commercial purpose punishable.

PROTECTIVE PROVISIONS:
S.19: Mandatory reporting — any person knowing of offence MUST report. Failure: up to 6 months.
S.23: Media prohibited from disclosing identity of child victim.
S.26: Child-friendly procedure — statement at child's residence; no repeat questioning.
S.28: Special Courts in every district for POCSO trials.
S.29: Presumption — if assault proved, court presumes it was without consent; burden shifts to accused.
S.35: Trial must complete within 1 year of taking cognizance.

PRACTICAL NOTES:
Q: What is domestic violence under PWDVA? A: Physical, sexual, verbal/emotional, and economic abuse.
Q: Can I file PWDVA without an FIR? A: Yes — PWDVA is a civil remedy.
Q: Who must report POCSO offences? A: Anyone with knowledge of the offence.
Q: What is the minimum punishment for penetrative sexual assault on a child? A: 10 years RI.
