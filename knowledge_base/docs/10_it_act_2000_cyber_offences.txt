=== INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY ACT, 2000 ===

CIVIL PENALTIES (S.43-43A):
S.43: Up to ₹1 crore for unauthorised computer access, downloading without permission, introducing virus, damaging data, disrupting computer systems.
S.43A: Body corporate negligent in maintaining security of sensitive personal data — liable to pay compensation (no cap).

CYBER OFFENCES:
S.65: Tampering with computer source code — 3 years imprisonment + ₹2 lakhs fine.
S.66: Computer-related offences (dishonest/fraudulent) — 3 years + ₹5 lakhs.
S.66A: Sending offensive messages — STRUCK DOWN by Supreme Court in Shreya Singhal v. UOI 2015 (unconstitutional — violated Art.19(1)(a)).
S.66B: Receiving stolen computer resources — 3 years + ₹1 lakh.
S.66C: Identity theft — 3 years + ₹1 lakh. Includes SIM swapping.
S.66D: Cheating by personation using computer (phishing, fake websites) — 3 years + ₹1 lakh.
S.66E: Violation of privacy — capturing/publishing private images without consent — 3 years + ₹2 lakhs.
S.66F: Cyber terrorism — LIFE IMPRISONMENT. Cognizable, non-bailable.
S.67: Publishing obscene material in electronic form — 3 years first, 5 years repeat.
S.67A: Sexually explicit material — 5 years first, 7 years repeat.
S.67B: Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) — 5 years first, 7 years repeat + ₹10 lakhs. Possession punishable.
S.70: Unauthorised access to protected critical infrastructure (power grids, financial systems) — 10 years.
S.72: Breach of confidentiality by intermediaries — 2 years + ₹1 lakh.

INTERMEDIARY LIABILITY (S.79):
Safe harbour: not liable if does not initiate, select or modify content and observes due diligence.
Safe harbour LOST when intermediary has actual knowledge of unlawful content and fails to remove it.
IT Rules 2021: Significant Social Media Intermediaries (5 million+ users) must appoint Grievance Officer (India-based, respond in 24 hours, resolve in 15 days).

DATA PROTECTION:
IT (SPDI Rules 2011): Consent required before collecting sensitive personal data (passwords, financial info, health, biometric).
Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDPA): Replaces SPDI rules. Data Protection Board established. Penalties up to ₹250 crore.

PRACTICAL NOTES:
Q: What is cyber terrorism under IT Act? A: S.66F — acts threatening India's unity/integrity through computer means; life imprisonment.
Q: Is sending offensive messages online illegal? A: S.66A was struck down in 2015. Other sections (66C, 66D, 66E) still apply.
Q: What is identity theft online? A: S.66C — using another's electronic signature, password, unique ID dishonestly.
Q: Are companies liable for data breaches? A: Yes under S.43A if negligent in maintaining reasonable security.
