=== RIGHT TO INFORMATION ACT, 2005 ===

DEFINITIONS (S.2):
Public authority (S.2(h)): Body constituted by Constitution, Parliament, State Legislature, or Government notification. Includes PSUs, nationalised banks, government-aided institutions, regulatory bodies (SEBI, RBI, IRDAI, TRAI).
Information (S.2(f)): Any material in any form — records, documents, memos, emails, opinions, data in electronic form.
Right to Information (S.2(j)): Inspection of work/documents; certified copies; certified samples; data in electronic format.

HOW TO FILE AN RTI (S.6):
Written application in English, Hindi, or official language of the area.
No prescribed format. No need to give reason.
Fees: Central Govt ₹10 application fee, ₹2/page copies, ₹5/hour inspection.
BPL applicants: completely exempt from all fees (S.7(5)).
File: in person, by post, or via RTI Online Portal (rtionline.gov.in).
If filed with wrong authority, PIO must transfer within 5 days (S.6(3)).

TIMELINES (S.7):
Normal: 30 days from receipt.
Life or liberty matters: 48 hours.
Transferred applications: 35 days from original receipt.
Deemed refusal if no response within 30 days.

FIRST APPEAL (S.19(1)): To First Appellate Authority within 30 days. Decision within 30-45 days.
SECOND APPEAL (S.19(3)): To CIC/SIC within 90 days. CIC/SIC can direct disclosure, impose penalty, recommend disciplinary action.

EXEMPTIONS (S.8):
(a) Sovereignty/security/strategic/economic interests of India.
(b) Court-ordered prohibition or contempt of court.
(c) Parliamentary privilege.
(d) Commercial confidence, trade secrets, third-party IP.
(e) Fiduciary relationship.
(f) Information received in confidence from foreign government.
(g) Life/physical safety endangerment.
(h) Impedes investigation/prosecution.
(i) Cabinet papers until decision taken.
(j) Personal information — unwarranted invasion of privacy.
Public interest override: S.8(2) — all exemptions can be overridden if public interest outweighs harm.

PENALTIES (S.20):
₹250/day for delay (maximum ₹25,000 per complaint).
CIC/SIC can recommend disciplinary action against PIO.
Burden on PIO to prove denial was justified.

PRACTICAL NOTES:
Q: Who can file an RTI? A: Any citizen of India.
Q: Do I need to give a reason? A: No reason required.
Q: What if PIO doesn't respond? A: Deemed refusal after 30 days — file First Appeal.
Q: Are intelligence agencies covered? A: Exempt except for allegations of corruption or human rights violations (S.24).
Q: Can RTI be filed online? A: Yes, at rtionline.gov.in.
