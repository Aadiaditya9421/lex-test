=== LIMITATION ACT, 1963 ===

S.3: Bar of limitation — every suit/appeal/application filed after prescribed period shall be dismissed even if limitation not raised by defendant.
S.4: If prescribed period expires on court holiday, it extends to next working day.
S.5: Condonation of delay — court may condone delay for appeals and applications (not suits) if sufficient cause shown.
S.6: Legal disability (minor, insane) — time runs from cessation of disability.
S.17: Fraud or mistake — limitation runs from discovery of fraud/mistake.
S.18: Fresh period from date of signed acknowledgement of liability.
S.19: Fresh period from date of part payment towards debt.

KEY LIMITATION PERIODS:
Money due on contract: 3 years.
Recovery of immoveable property: 12 years.
Execution of decree: 12 years.
Compensation for personal injury: 3 years.
Compensation for libel/slander: 1 year.
Consumer complaint (CP Act 2019): 2 years.
High Court appeal from District Court: 90 days.
First civil appeal: 30 days from decree.

Criminal limitation (CrPC S.468):
Fine only offence: 6 months.
Up to 1 year imprisonment: 1 year.
Up to 3 years imprisonment: 3 years.
Above 3 years: no bar on cognizance.

=== LEGAL GLOSSARY ===

Accused: Person charged with a criminal offence; not yet convicted.
Acquittal: Court's finding that accused is not guilty; bars re-trial for same offence.
Affidavit: Written statement of facts sworn before an oath commissioner.
Anticipatory Bail: Bail obtained before arrest under CrPC S.438.
Bail: Temporary release of accused from custody on furnishing security.
Bailable Offence: Offence where bail is a right (listed as bailable in 1st Schedule CrPC).
Cause of Action: Facts giving rise to legal right to sue; determines limitation period.
Cognizable Offence: Offence where police can arrest without warrant.
Compoundable Offence: Offence that can be settled between parties (S.320 CrPC).
Conviction: Court's finding that accused is guilty.
Default Bail: Bail under CrPC S.167(2) when chargesheet not filed in time.
Double Jeopardy: Cannot be tried twice for same offence (Art.20, CrPC S.300).
Ex Parte: Order passed in absence of one party.
FIR (First Information Report): Information given to police about cognizable offence (CrPC S.154).
Grievous Hurt: Eight categories under IPC S.320 — fractures, permanent loss of limb/organ, etc.
Habeas Corpus: Writ directing production of detained person before court.
Injunction: Court order to do or refrain from doing an act.
Jurisdiction: Court's authority to hear — territorial, pecuniary, subject matter.
Magistrate: Judicial officer who tries petty cases; commits serious cases to Sessions Court.
Mandamus: Writ commanding public authority to perform public duty.
Mens Rea: Criminal intention; required for most offences.
Non-Bailable Offence: Offence where bail is discretionary, not a right.
Non-Cognizable Offence: Police need magistrate's order to arrest.
Prima Facie: On first appearance — sufficient evidence to proceed.
Remand: Sending accused into custody by Magistrate pending investigation.
Sessions Court: Principal criminal court of a district.
Suo Motu: Court takes action on its own motion without application.
Writ: Written order by High Court (Art.226) or Supreme Court (Art.32).
Zero FIR: FIR filed at any police station regardless of territorial jurisdiction; transferred afterwards.

PRACTICAL NOTES:
Q: What is the limitation period for filing a money recovery suit? A: 3 years.
Q: Can delay be condoned for suits? A: No — S.5 applies only to appeals and applications.
Q: What is the limitation for a consumer complaint? A: 2 years from cause of action.
Q: What is a Zero FIR? A: FIR filed at any station; must be transferred to jurisdictional station.
